# Reader Q&A: The Short Answer

**Question**: Why would UK institutions with GBP liabilities hold USD stablecoins? And why abandon gilt yields for zero-yield stablecoins?

---

## The Direct Answer

You're right to be skeptical. **Under current conditions, our argument is weaker than we suggested.**

### On FX Risk

The threat doesn't come from pension funds (they won't hold USD). It comes from:
- **Multinational corporations** with USD revenues (natural hedge, not a currency mismatch)
- **Asset managers** with no fixed liabilities
- **Retail savers** who don't understand currency risk

UK multinationals hold ~£200-300 billion in USD working capital. Even a 20% shift to Agentic USD = £40-60 billion drain from the domestic banking system.

### On Yield

**You've identified a real gap.** GENIUS currently prohibits yield pass-through, so our argument doesn't hold.

However: **GENIUS will likely be amended.** Why?
- Competition with CBDCs (digital euro, digital pound)
- Market demand from institutions
- No technical barrier

Once yield is permitted, Agentic USD (4.5-5% yield, 24/7 settlement, programmable) becomes superior to gilts (4-5% yield, 2-3 day settlement, illiquid).

### On Threat to Major Economies

**Honest assessment**: Threat is lower for major economies than emerging markets.

**But it's real through three channels:**

1. **Corporate treasury drain** - Multinationals shift USD working capital to Agentic USD
2. **Velocity problem** - High-velocity Agentic USD creates outsized economic effects despite small volume
3. **Systemic risk** - If Agentic USD becomes the backstop for global insurance (as we argue in Article 8), a crisis in Agentic USD becomes a global crisis

**Timeline:**
- **2025-2027**: Low threat (no yield pass-through)
- **2027-2028**: GENIUS amended, threat increases
- **2028-2030**: Threat becomes significant

---

## Bottom Line

You're correct: the threat to major economies is **not imminent**. But it's **real and growing**, contingent on GENIUS amendment and Agentic USD adoption by institutions.

For policymakers: Act now or face a problem in 3-5 years.
